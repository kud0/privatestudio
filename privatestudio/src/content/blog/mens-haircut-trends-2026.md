## The trends that will dominate 2026

Men's barbering is constantly evolving. In 2026, we're seeing a clear shift toward styles that combine **classic elegance with contemporary touches**. At Private Studio Barcelona, we analyze the main trends and how to adapt them to each face type.

## 1. The Textured Low Fade

The low fade remains king, but with an important evolution: **natural texture**. The goal is no longer a rigid, perfect finish — it's a more organic look with movement.

**Best for**: Oval and square faces.

## 2. The Old Money Aesthetic

Inspired by timeless elegance, this style features:
- A defined side part
- Hair combed back or to the side
- A polished yet natural finish
- No extreme fades

It's the perfect cut for the professional man looking to project seriousness and sophistication.

**Best for**: Rectangular and oval faces.

## 3. The Modern Mullet

The mullet is back, but reinvented. More subtle and stylized than its 80s version, the modern mullet combines short sides with length at the back and plenty of texture.

**Best for**: Round and triangular faces.

## 4. The Buzz Cut with Personality

The buzz cut is back in force, but with details like:
- Subtle design lines
- Creative fades
- A well-crafted complementary beard

**Best for**: Faces with a defined jawline.

## 5. Medium Length with Movement

For those who prefer longer hair, the trend is a **layered cut that enhances natural movement**. It's worked 100% with scissors for a softer finish.

**Best for**: Diamond and heart-shaped faces.

## The role of beards in 2026 trends

The beard continues to play a leading role. Trends point toward:
- **Well-shaped medium beards**: Neither too long nor stubble. The sweet spot.
- **Beard-to-hair gradient**: Smooth, natural transitions.
- **Statement mustache**: The mustache is back as a style accent.
